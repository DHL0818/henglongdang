I served as a student representative in Bellevue School District's Strategic Plan Coalition, contributing to discussions on long-range planning, equity-centered implementation, and practical measurement design. My role focused on translating student experience into actionable planning signals while helping connect high-level strategy to day-to-day systems decisions.

Goals, Process, and My Contribution

  • Goals & Measures: I contributed most in conversations where quantitative targets needed qualitative context (for example: belonging, participation, access, and student-reported barriers), which matches my broader systems-and-human factors profile.
  • Our Process: The coalition's inclusive design process (students, caregivers, educators, and community leaders) mirrors how I approach strategy work: participatory diagnosis first, then measurable interventions.
  • Significance of participation: My value was not ownership of the entire plan, but helping bridge perspectives—linking technical framing, policy language, and lived student experience so implementation choices remain both rigorous and humane.
